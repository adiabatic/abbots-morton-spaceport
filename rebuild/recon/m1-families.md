## 1. qsPea from scratch (U+E650, tall, 9-row bitmaps)

### 1.1 Ductus, verbatim

`glyph_data/quikscript.yaml` lines 344–360 (the design's "trails off mid-sentence" entry; the final bullet ends with a trailing space after "but" and two blank lines, and "recieve" is misspelled in the source):

```text
Standard (full):

- Begin at the left, slightly above the x-height (occasionally, another letter will join at the entry). Rise to the top and rightwards in a clockwise curve. Then pull straight down on the right toward the baseline. It may connect to the next letter at the baseline.

Half:

- As in the standard way, but the downstroke stops at the x-height, or just above it, where it connects to the next letter.

Dipping down to the x-height:

- As in the standard way, but the left upward stroke begins at the x-height to recieve a join from a previous letter that exits at the x-height.

Dipping down to the x-height on both sides:

- As in the half way, but
```

The unfinished fourth way describes the `half_dips_down_to_xheight` shape (the both-sides-dipped half used in the (en-y5, ex-y5) cell). Note the ductus already encodes the en-y6 convention: the standard entry is "slightly above the x-height" — that is the `[0, 6]` entry anchor.

### 1.5 Proposed way/stance shape (for the authoring agent, not binding)

Two stances — `full` (the standard way) and `half` — matching the ductus's first two ways. The two "dipping" ways collapse into bindings per §4's check 2 (each dip is a 1-pixel same-row edit at an attachment row): full's x-height entry row binds `dips_down_to_short_height` (or a `stub: {cols: [0], when: withdrawn}`), half's x-height exit row binds the right-dip (stub col 3), and the (en-y5, ex-y5) half cell explicitly binds `half_dips_down_to_xheight` (the §3.2 two-side-bindings-compose case — and the cell whose ductus sentence was never finished). The full surface needs entry rows at y6, y5 (scoped `from:` {qsMay ex-y5, qsUtter ex-y5}), y0 (scoped `from:` {qsEt, qsAwe}; see §6.2), and exit rows at y0; the half surface needs entry rows at y6 and y5, exit rows at y6 (scoped `toward:` qsPea en-y6), and y5 (with the `exit_ink_y: 6` / dipped-ink duality). The ductus rewrite must finish the fourth sentence and decide whether "half, dipping on both sides" stays an enumerated way (then a `cells:` binding realizes it) — it must not become a stance, since the pen motion is the half motion.

## 2. The §4 classification tables

Verdict vocabulary: SHAPE (survives as a stance), ANCHOR (becomes a cell of a surviving stance), SUPPRESS (becomes `refuse`), CONTEXT (becomes `prefer` / a row scope / nothing — emergent), SS (becomes an `unlocks` row with context intact), EXT (becomes an `extend`/`contract` record, parametric or `bind:`-bound). DEAD = mechanism category that ceases to exist (§1 of the design). Suggested stance names are illustrative; the linter bans `(before|after|noentry|noexit|nonjoining|ss[0-9])` in stance IDs, so none of today's modifier-flavored names can carry over.

## 3. Ligatures

All 13 ligature families today: qsTea_qsOy, qsDay_qsUtter, qsDay_qsEat, qsThey_qsZoo, qsThey_qsUtter, qsVie_qsUtter, qsSee_qsUtter, qsSee_qsEat, qsJai_qsUtter, qsJay_qsUtter, qsWay_qsUtter, qsWhy_qsUtter, qsOut_qsTea. Formation is unconditional type-4 GSUB for all (design §5.7).

Exactly two have a component in {qsIt, qsTea, qsPea, qsMay} — none involve qsPea, qsIt, or qsMay:

- **`qsTea_qsOy`** (lead qsTea): prop bitmap with `exit: [8, 0]` only. Entrylessness is _emergent_ (entry inheritance from the lead fails because the ligature's leftmost ink at y0/y8 doesn't match qsTea's x=0 anchors) — the rune file should declare an explicit empty `entries: {}` so the obligation is visible (prototype recon §5). No `.noentry` twin compiles. Forces predecessor withdrawal: ·It goes fully bare, ·May lands in its exit-anchor-only bare form, ·Excite is the `_PENDING_LIGA_ENTRY_GUARDS` worked case (out of M1 scope). With ss03 active and an x-height-exit predecessor, the gated half-Tea substitution consumes qsTea before the liga lookup and **no ligature forms** — the formation-vs-marker staging decision §7 of the design already settles (markers run after formation), so this is an expected intended divergence to record against the baseline.
- **`qsOut_qsTea`** (trailing qsTea): entry (0,0) inherited from qsOut, **no exit**; `after_see` stance with its own hand-drawn bitmap (the §3.3 `bind:` example), a derived `en-trim-1`, and a `.noentry` twin. Lead qsOut is outside the M1 alphabet, so it cannot form in M1 windows; its only M1-relevant effect is selector expansion — followers' `after: [{family: qsTea}]` clauses pick it up as a trailing-component expansion today, which the new model re-derives automatically.

`noentry_after` records on ligatures targeting M1 families (the §13.5-relevant subtlety — these live on the ligature, kill the seam from the right, and today trigger the entry-preserving `ex-noentry` machinery on the left):

- `qsDay_qsEat.prop.derive.noentry_after: [{family: qsIt}]` — after ·It the ligature refuses its entry; pairs with `qsIt.entry_baseline_exit_noentry_before_day_exam`.
- `qsThey_qsUtter.prop.derive.noentry_after: […, {family: qsMay}]` — the §5.1 `·May·They+Utter` worked pair; pairs with qsMay's withdrawal binding.

In the new format both become refuse rows on the ligature rune (`refuse: [{entry: baseline, when: {left: {family: qsIt}}}]`-shaped), and the predecessor's withdrawal is pure cell semantics.

**Closure analysis and recommendation.** Within the strict M1 alphabet {qsPea, qsTea, qsIt, qsMay} ∪ {space, ZWNJ, namer dot}, **no digraph forms a ligature** — I checked every ordered pair against the 13 `sequence:` declarations (the only sequences touching the four are (qsTea, qsOy) and (qsOut, qsTea), and neither qsOy nor qsOut is in the four). So the minimal alphabet is closed under formation with zero ligatures modeled, and oracle conformance is achievable without any ligature work. However, M1 should follow the prototype and include **qsTea_qsOy**, because the entryless-ligature predecessor-withdrawal seam is the single most architecture-proving behavior in the subset (§5.7) and the prototype already hand-encoded it. That forces a decision about qsOy:

1. **Admit qsOy as a fully modeled fifth rune** (recommended). qsOy is small — bare + one `entry_xheight` stance (en-y5, ex-y0; `after:` {qsGay ex-y5, qsFee ex-y5, qsMay ex-y5, …} with extend_entry_after ← halves) + noentry twin — and the subset then gains real windows it must conform on anyway once qsOy is typeable: ·May·Oy joins (qsOy's from-scope includes qsMay ex-y5), ·Pea·Oy breaks (baseline-verified), Tea·Oy forms. This keeps the conformance gate total over the alphabet, with no carve-outs to explain.
2. Admit qsOy as formation-input-only and restrict the conformance basis to windows where qsOy appears only immediately after qsTea. Cheaper but leaves a documented hole in "oracle conformance over the subset alphabet," and the restriction machinery itself is new code.

Do **not** pull in qsOut/qsOut_qsTea for M1; its interesting edge (the after-·See bound shape) needs qsSee too, and its trailing-Tea expansion effects are out of reach of the M1 alphabet.
